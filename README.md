# academic-research
# Government Expenditure & GDP Growth in Kenya (2013–2020)

Undergraduate research project examining how Kenyan government spending on the 
Big Four Agenda sectors — health, agriculture, manufacturing, and construction — 
affected economic growth between 2013 and 2020.

**Method:** Multiple regression analysis in SPSS, using time-series data from KNBS, 
CBK, the World Bank, and the IMF, with full diagnostic testing (linearity, 
heteroscedasticity, normality) to validate the model.

**Key finding:** The model explained 92.4% of the variance in GDP (R² = 0.924); 
all four sectors showed a positive, statistically significant relationship with growth.

Co-authored with a 5-person team, supervised by Dr. Joseph Nzomoi, Department of 
Economics, South Eastern Kenya University (2022).
